Rating scale

Each dimension uses a 1–5 scale. The overall question for each dimension is in italics.

Size of Customer Base

How big do you believe the reach of this channel is?
1 tiny — very few users 2 small — niche audience 3 moderate — sizeable but not mainstream 4 large — widely adopted 5 massive — ubiquitous

Complexity

How hard do you think this channel is to build for and operate?
1 very simple — off-the-shelf, minimal integration 2 simple — mostly straightforward 3 moderate — some integration work and tradeoffs 4 complex — significant engineering effort 5 highly complex — major investment to do well

Expectations of Correctness

How much do users tolerate errors on this channel?
1 mistakes are fine — casual context, errors expected 2 errors mostly forgiven — low-stakes interactions 3 errors noticed but accepted — users will retry 4 errors frustrate users — they expect things to work 5 zero errors expected — mistakes break trust

Expectations of Richness

How rich or interactive does the experience need to be on this channel?
1 plain text only — text in/text out 2 light formatting — basic media, simple lists 3 mixed text + images — cards, inline images 4 images and structured UI — buttons, carousels 5 rich media / interactive UI — full app-like experience

ARR Potential (per Unit)

How much annual recurring revenue do you think each unit of this channel can generate?
1 minimal — rounding error 2 low 3 moderate 4 significant 5 very high — anchor revenue

Incremental vs. Cannibal

Does this channel grow the pie or take orders that would have happened elsewhere?
1 fully cannibalizes — pure substitution 2 mostly cannibal — some new demand, mostly displacement 3 mixed — roughly half new, half displaced 4 mostly incremental — mostly new demand 5 fully incremental — all-new revenue
